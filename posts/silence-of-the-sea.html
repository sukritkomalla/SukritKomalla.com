Silence of the Sea

The waves rewrite the sand,
a cold hand washing over old stone.
Words left unspoken in the salt-stung wind,
dissolve like white foam on the shore.

We watch the lighthouse blink,
a pulse in the dark, counting out the hours,
measuring the distance between tide and time,
where the land ends and the silence starts.

Underneath, the deep currents hum,
carrying secrets of ancient ships,
while we stand at the edge,
listening to the quiet weight of the water.
